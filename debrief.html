Study 327 Debrief

Please read through the debrief below and then click on the button at the end to confirm you have been debriefed. Note that you must click on the button at the bottom of the page to acknowledge you have received debriefing information in order to receive your credit on Sona.

  1. What kind of study is it, e.g., descriptive, correlational?

    Experimental

  2. What are the independent variables? How are you operationalizing them?

    The independent variables are the availability of alternative causes you were presented with. You were randomly allocated into one of two groups: a hidden alternative or no alternative.

  3. What are the dependent variables that were measured? How are you operationalizing them?

    The dependent variable was your belief that the target cue caused the shock, operationalised by your responses to causal rating questions at the end of the experiment.

  4. What is one potential confounding variable and how have you attempted to control for it?

    A potential confounding variable is that participants had pre-existing biases about cues and their outcomes (e.g. the colour red may be associated with danger). We tried to control for this by setting up a cover story and instructing you to only use information from within the experiment to make your causal judgments.

  5. What is one potential ethical issue, and how has the experimenter attempted to resolve it?

    Participants might believe that the experiment is a test of their intelligence. We have attempted to resolve this by emphasising that there are no correct answers and we are only interested in what and how participants learn.

  6. How did the experimenter get from first year psychology to conducting this research?

    I did my undergraduate in Finance and Psychology, going on to complete my honours in Psychology at UNSW. I have gone on to do a PhD which this research is for.
